Storing Vinyl Records

Where to Store Vinyl Records

When it comes to storing your vinyl records, the biggest decision you need to answer is where you’re going to store those records—either in the long- or short-term. This means considering the exposure to light, humidity and temperature and the type of storage you’re using. Always store your vinyl upright. Storing them horizontally can cause ring wear and warping over time.

  • Temperature

    If you didn’t already know, vinyl records are made of a type of plastic called polyvinyl chloride (PVC). This type of plastic basically lasts forever and is generally protective against the elements, which is why it’s used in everything from window frames to wire insulation.
    However, PVC has a major weak spot—heat. At 140 °F (60 °C), PVC will start to experience “heat distortion.” In other words, that’s the temperature wherein your vinyl records will start to warp.

    Unless you’re in a major heat wave without air conditioning, it’s pretty unlikely that an occupied area of your house will hit this temperature. After all, that kind of heat is dangerous for humans, too. But poorly insulated garages, attics, storage units, car trunks, sheds or even sunrooms can creep up to 140 °F.

    When in doubt, invest in a WiFi thermometer that can send an app notification to your phone and try to keep your records in an environment between 65° to 70° F. Bonus points if it has a hygrometer that alerts you when humidity l evels are too high or low—more on that later.

  • Humidity & Water

    The ideal climate for vinyl records is between 45% to 50% humidity. Anything more humid can attract mildew and mold. Mold buildup in the grooves of your records can make them completely unplayable. Your paper record sleeves can also get moldy and fall apart.

    Remember to keep your vinyl in a cool, dry place with good air circulation. Avoid basements, attics, and garages where humidity levels can fluctuate significantly. Also, keep them away from water heaters or pipes that could potentiallly leak over your collection.

  • Sunlight

    Do not store your record collection in an area that sees too much sunlight. Unfortunately, sunlight creates additional heat and contains UV rays. Direct sunlight will warm your vinyl, increasing the temperature and making it susceptible to warping.

    UV rays will damage everything from skin to paint on album artwork. By keeping your albums away from sunlight, youll keep your album artwork as vibrant as the day it entered your life!

Cleaning Your Records and the Sytlus

Your vinyl records are probably unclean after sitting around or bringing them home from the wild. New records are staticky and attract dust and also require cleaning. When properly cared for, your vinyl record collectiion will last a lifetime and beyond. Most importantly, clean vinyl records sound amazing. So how do how do you go about cleaning your records?

You'll need some basic supplies including:

  • Microfiber Cleaning Cloths Soft, lint-free cloths for wiping the records dry.

  • Record Cleaning Solution A mild cleaning solution to remove dirt and other contaminants.

  • Anti-static Brush To remove dust and reduce static buildup.

  • Soft Bristle Brush To clean records before applying the cleaning solution.

  • Distilled Water To combine with cleaning solution for effective cleaning.

Here are the steps to clean your vinyl records:

  1. Remove Dust and Debris

  2. Apply cleaning solution

  3. Swipe the record's surface

  4. Dry the record
