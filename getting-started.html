History of
Vinyl Records

Vinyl’s story began in the early 1900s, from fragile shellac discs to the long-playing records introduced in 1948. Through the 1950s to the 1980s, vinyl defined how people listened to music. Though CDs and digital streaming nearly replaced it, vinyl never truly faded. Thanks to collectors, artists, and fans, it’s made a powerful comeback, proving that great sound and classic style never go out of fashion.
